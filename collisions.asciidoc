[[chp.collisions]]
== Physics Models Used in Modelling Collisions
include::stylesheets/Toggle[]

[[sec.collisions.intro]]
=== Introduction

opalx-begin

This chapter documents the current collision model used by the `BEAMBEAM`
element in _OPALX_.

The present implementation is a reduced symmetric head-on collision
model. It provides the interaction-window field solve and its
associated mesh handling without yet introducing two independently
tracked bunch objects.

The current model therefore uses:

1. one physical bunch, represented by the actual particle container,
2. one virtual bunch, reconstructed from the physical bunch by symmetry about
  the interaction point,
3. an interaction-window field solve that is active only while the bunch
  traverses the interval defined by the element length `L`.

The model is entered when the head of the physical bunch reaches the
beginning of the interaction window and is left once the tail of the
bunch has passed the end of the window.
opalx-end


[[sec.collisions.ansatz]]
=== Symmetric Counter-Propagating Ansatz

opalx-begin
Let latexmath:[z_\mathrm{IP}] denote the interaction-point position in
the local beam coordinate system during the interaction-window solve.
For a particle of the physical bunch with coordinates
latexmath:[(x,y,z)] and momentum latexmath:[(p_x,p_y,p_z)], the
virtual counter-propagating partner bunch is constructed by the
symmetry transformation

[latexmath]
++++
\begin{aligned}
x'   &= x, \\
y'   &= y, \\
z'   &= 2 z_\mathrm{IP} - z,
\end{aligned}
\qquad
\begin{aligned}
p'_x &= p_x, \\
p'_y &= p_y, \\
p'_z &= -p_z.
\end{aligned}
++++

This is the current head-on, fully symmetric ansatz. It assumes that
the two bunches are identical up to the longitudinal mirror operation
about the interaction point.
opalx-end

[[sec.collisions.mesh-model]]
=== Mesh Model
opalx-begin
Outside the interaction window, the longitudinal field mesh remains
bunch-following. In software-engineering terms, this is a
Lagrangian-in-z representation of the field mesh.

Inside the interaction window, the field mesh changes its meaning:

* in latexmath:[x] and latexmath:[y], the mesh remains bunch-following,
* in latexmath:[z], the mesh is frozen over the full interaction window.

The longitudinal field mesh therefore becomes Eulerian in
latexmath:[z], while the physical bunch itself continues to move
through that fixed window. This distinction is important:

* physical bunch bounds remain the actual particle bounds,
* field-domain bounds in latexmath:[z] represent the fixed interaction window.
opalx-end

[[sec.collisions.scope]]
=== Scope of the Current Model

opalx-begin

The present implementation should be interpreted as a first collision
model for symmetric head-on beams. It is suitable for studying:

* entry into and exit from the interaction window,
* the switch from a bunch-following to a frozen longitudinal field mesh,
* the self-field solve on that interaction-window mesh,
* the symmetry-based reconstruction of the opposing bunch.

It is not yet a full two-bunch strong-strong model with two independently
tracked particle populations.
opalx-end
